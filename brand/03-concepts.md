# Divergent Ideation — market-data-bridge

## Concept A: "The Join"

**Metaphor**: Two data columns aligning and merging — like a SQL JOIN or schema alignment. Three input streams (representing IBKR, Yahoo, CSV) converge into a single unified output row.

**Visual**: Three parallel vertical lines on the left, narrowing through a structured connection point, emerging as a single enriched line on the right. The connection point is geometric and precise — not a funnel, but an alignment grid.

**Why it's specific**: Directly maps to what the software does — ParsedContent normalization, multi-source quote routing, ensemble scoring. A JOIN is the exact operation.

**Why it might be generic**: "Lines merging" could be any integration product. Must be executed with enough structural detail to feel like data, not just "convergence."

**Favicon survival**: The three-to-one structure reads clearly even at 16x16 as three short strokes meeting one.

### Variants:
- **A1**: Three lines → grid node → one line (horizontal flow)
- **A2**: Three vertical columns with a horizontal bridge bar connecting them (like a schema mapping diagram)
- **A3**: Three arrows pointing inward to a center square, which outputs a single enriched arrow

---

## Concept B: "The Bridge Span"

**Metaphor**: An abstract structural bridge — not a literal bridge, but the engineering concept of spanning a gap. Two pillars (data sources / AI consumers) connected by a structured span (the protocol layer).

**Visual**: Two squared uprights with a precise horizontal connector between them. The connector has internal structure — tick marks, grid lines, or data-point indicators — showing it's not empty but carrying structured data across.

**Why it's specific**: The product literally bridges brokerage APIs to AI tool interfaces. The "span" contains the enrichment logic. The structural detail in the span differentiates it from generic connection marks.

**Why it might be generic**: Any "platform" or "integration" company could use a bridge metaphor. Needs the internal data-structure detail to be specific.

**Favicon survival**: Two pillars + connector is extremely simple and legible at any size.

### Variants:
- **B1**: Two uprights with a structured beam (tick marks along the span)
- **B2**: Two brackets `[ ]` with a dashed/structured line between them — feels more "code"
- **B3**: Two vertical bars with three horizontal connecting bars (like an "H" with multiple rungs — representing multiple data channels)

---

## Concept C: "The Ensemble Grid"

**Metaphor**: The 3-model ensemble evaluation. Three parallel scoring tracks feed into a weighted consensus. This is unique to market-data-bridge — no other product has this exact three-model eval architecture.

**Visual**: A 3-column micro-grid where each column represents a model (GPT, Claude, Gemini), with an overlay or bottom bar showing the weighted ensemble result. Looks like a compact data visualization — a micro heatmap or scoring matrix.

**Why it's specific**: This is the most differentiated feature of the product. No other MCP bridge has a 3-model eval engine. The visual directly represents the architecture diagram from SYSTEM_CARD.md.

**Why it might be generic**: Could look like a generic "analytics" icon. Needs the 3-column structure to be unmistakable, not just a random grid.

**Favicon survival**: A 3-column mini-grid is inherently simple. Very strong at small sizes — reads like a compact data widget.

### Variants:
- **C1**: Three vertical bars of varying height with a horizontal baseline (like a micro bar chart, but structured)
- **C2**: 3×3 grid with the bottom row highlighted (ensemble consensus)
- **C3**: Three dots/nodes at top converging to a single bar at bottom (scoring → consensus)

---

## Concept D: "The Protocol Mark"

**Metaphor**: The dual-protocol nature of the bridge — MCP (stdio, structured) and REST (HTTP, OpenAPI). Two distinct interface patterns that speak to the same data.

**Visual**: Two interlocking geometric shapes — one representing the structured/typed MCP interface, one representing the REST/HTTP interface. They share a common data core.

**Why it's specific**: The dual-protocol architecture (MCP + REST serving identical data) is architecturally distinctive. The mark literally shows two interfaces to one truth.

**Why it might be generic**: "Two shapes interlocking" is abstract enough to mean anything. Needs the interface/protocol framing to land.

**Favicon survival**: Two simple interlocking shapes can work at 16x16 if the shapes are distinct enough.

### Variants:
- **D1**: A square (MCP/structured) overlapping a circle (REST/open) with shared fill in the overlap
- **D2**: Two brackets — angle bracket `<>` and curly bracket `{}` — interlocked (representing different protocol syntaxes)
- **D3**: Two parallel lines with different internal patterns (dashed vs solid) meeting at a shared endpoint

---

## Concept E: "The Enrichment Pipeline"

**Metaphor**: Raw data entering thin and simple, passing through processing stages, emerging thick and enriched. Like the 14 deterministic features being computed: raw quote → ATR, RSI, RVOL, gap, spread, VWAP → scored, weighted, regime-tagged output.

**Visual**: A left-to-right progression from a thin line/simple shape to a richer, more detailed form. Stages of enrichment visible in the mark.

**Why it's specific**: The feature computation pipeline (14 features from raw data) is core to the eval engine. This shows the "value-add" of the bridge — data doesn't just pass through, it gets enriched.

**Why it might be generic**: "Simple → complex" is a common visual metaphor. Needs the specific data/grid language to feel like market data enrichment, not generic "transformation."

**Favicon survival**: A two-stage or three-stage progression can read at small sizes if abstracted enough, but risks becoming muddy.

### Variants:
- **E1**: Thin line → medium line → thick line with tick marks (horizontal, like a growing data bar)
- **E2**: Small open square → medium square with internal grid → filled/structured square
- **E3**: Arrow from thin to thick with vertical "enrichment bars" crossing it

| D: The Protocol Mark | Medium — architectural | Good | Medium | Abstract, hard to decode |
| E: The Pipeline | Medium — process metaphor | Weak | Medium | Generic "transformation" |

**Strongest candidates for asset creation**: A (The Join), B (The Bridge Span), C (The Ensemble Grid)

**Kill early**: D feels too abstract to decode. E has weak favicon survival.

**Recommended focus**: Combine the best of A and C — the convergence/join idea with the 3-column ensemble structure. This gives us both the "bridge" meaning and the distinctive 3-model architecture.
